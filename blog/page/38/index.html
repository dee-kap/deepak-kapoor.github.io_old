Oslo Developer Center Is Live on MSDN

Oct 31st, 2008 | Comments

An exciting news I found while reading Marquee de Sells is the launch of Oslo Developer Center. I have been following Oslo story for sometime now and I have to say that information has only trickled so far. Oslo Developer Center is the first step in taking the covers off Oslo and educating the masses about it with a flow of information. Microsoft has not committed to the release date/period for Oslo but given that teams have been working on it for what seems to be a long time, it should not be too far (I am speculating here). If you are interesting in learning more about Oslo then may I recommend that you start with Oslo FAQs which answers important questions such as What is Oslo? The answer (partially taken from Oslo FAQ) is:
“Oslo” is the code name for our platform for model-driven applications. The goal of “Oslo” is to provide a 10x productivity gain by making model-driven applications mainstream
10x productivity! You’ve got my attention.

Word 2007 Cannot Click on Document

Oct 30th, 2008 | Comments

I have encountered an issue with Word 2007 many times. The issue is that when I open a document, I cannot click anywhere within the document. I can however click on the ribbon, status bar and the chrome but somehow not on the document. I also cannot select anything in my document. The solution to fix this issue is documented at Microsoft Help and Support. I am somehow confident that I will get this issue again. And this post will save my time searching for the solution.

Free LINQ to SQL Training in Sydney

I am running a LINQ To SQL Training day on Saturday November 8th at Microsoft Office in North Ryde. The course is FREE for anyone to attend but there are only 20 places available.
